The WordGirl website is specially designed to promote a love of language and words while extending the basic principals of The Amazing Colossal Adventures of WordGirl. Each exciting game and activity will expose children to new and empowering words in an appealing way. The connection between language and thinking that children gain by building a broad vocabulary has a direct impact on their literacy learning, especially reading comprehension. Our goal is that your children will find themselves using new words in daily conversation and develop a love of language.

The WordGirl website is a giant game in and of itself. We want kids to feel they are actually in the world of WordGirl where they can control and play within various elements of her world. The web experience stays true to the television program. For example, Huggy’s House of Fun is designed to feel like an actual amusement park. Beyond the main activities, there are other classic mini-games such as “Dunk Huggy” or “Test Your Strength.”

GAMES

Captain Huggy Face's Freeze Frame!
In this fill-in-the-blank sentence game, kids watch an episode of WordGirl. At key moments the episode will pause on a particular frame. A sentence missing one word will pop-up to describe the scene or characters. There are 4 word options from which to choose in order to correctly complete the sentence. The choices are engaging and unambiguous. Kids will find it amusing to try out each word and eliminate the more absurd selections. This game teaches children new words as they are commonly used within context.

Maze Mania
Help WordGirl fly through a maze as she literally uncovers words with the same meanings.

Huggy Dance
Huggy is asked to show the meaning a word, his facial expressions. If the player is correct, Huggy does a victory dance.

Power Words
Power Words challenges kids to find words whose definitions can be seen visually. Each question shows WordGirl and Captain Huggy Face in various sticky predicaments. The situation is presented to the users, who in turn choose the best word to help WordGirl save the day. Power Words concretely demonstrates the importance and power of a new word by virtue of its necessity to the action of the game.

Comic Strip
WordGirl stars in her very own classic superhero comic strip where kids help choose her adventure. The outcome of the comic strip is determined by which word the use selects. The comic strip game is constantly changing and it is up to the children to help WordGirl and Captain Huggy Face outsmart the villains. When the story comes to an end, students can print their comic strips. This game is a chance for children to feel masterful over language and story telling and will be particularly appealing to kids who may be struggling with reading and writing.

Message Maker Game
Click on a word, drag it into a crystal ball to make a message and send to a friend.
