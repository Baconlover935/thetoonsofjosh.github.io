Activity: Keepsake Scrapbook

Age Range: 3-7
Clifford's Big Idea to Grow On: Believe in Yourself
Skills:
• Language and Literacy Skills
• Social and Emotional Skills
• Understanding and Appreciating Diversity
Related Books:
• The Mixed-Up Chameleon by Eric Carle (Harper Trophy)
• The Keeping Quilt by Patricia Polaco (Simon & Schuster Children's)

Create a keepsake scrapbook together that will chronicle your child's heritage, growth, activities, interests, etc.

Materials:

  • family photos and materials for starting your child's scrapbook

Directions:
Start a scrapbook of memories to enjoy for years to come. A keepsake scrapbook can be made from many things and doesn't have to be fancy. Try using a simple three-ring binder, or handy folder. Begin your child's keepsake scrapbook by sharing the following ideas with your child:

  • Baby Days- Discover, collect and record things about your child's miraculous birth!
  • Toddler Days- Discover, collect and record events like family gatherings, changes in family, birthday parties, friends, preschool, pets, etc.
  • Here and Now- Discover, collect and record favorite cartoons, books, music, movies, games, toys, foods, friends, pets, sports, physical growth, places to go, future interests, etc.
  • From Past to Present- Discover, collect and record information about your child's heritage.

Talk about It: Ask your child to share what he/she finds most interesting about family members and special family traditions. Talk about how keeping a family album or special

Take it Further: Introduce your child to family traditions by sharing old or favorite family recipes, songs, games, etc. Then provide opportunities for your child to invite a friend over to share these family traditions with. Be sure to invite your child's friend (via parent or caregiver) to also share family traditions that the two friends could experience together.

With a Group: Invite each group member to bring something from their family's heritage to share with the group. This would be a great time to invite family member's to come and share their childhood memories, traditional foods, art, and family traditions.
